Security Tips

Practical security habits make all the difference. Follow these tips to protect yourself, your family, and your property in Edenvale, Kempton Park, Benoni, Primrose and beyond.

Personal Safety Home Security Emergency Contact

Personal Safety

Staying safe in public and private spaces

  • Stay Aware of Surroundings

    Always be conscious of your environment. Avoid distractions like phones when walking in public spaces.

  • Trust Your Instincts

    If a situation feels unsafe, leave immediately. Your intuition is a powerful safety tool.

  • Keep Emergency Contacts Accessible

    Store emergency numbers in your phone and have them written down in case of battery failure.

  • Share Plans Wisely

    Avoid sharing travel plans or daily routines publicly on social media.

Home Security

Protecting your home and family

  • Lock Doors & Windows

    Always lock all entry points, even when you're at home. Use deadbolts for added security.

  • Install Security Lighting

    Motion-activated lights around your property deter potential intruders and improve visibility.

  • Hide Valuables

    Keep expensive items out of sight from windows. Use safes for important documents and valuables.

  • Test Alarm Systems

    Regularly test your alarm system and ensure batteries are fresh. Know how to operate it properly.

  • Secure Perimeter

    Maintain fences, gates, and walls. Trim shrubs that could provide hiding spots near windows.

Vehicle Safety

Keeping your vehicle secure

  • Always Lock Your Vehicle

    Lock doors immediately upon entry and exit, even for quick stops.

  • Park in Well-Lit Areas

    Choose busy, well-lit parking spots. Avoid isolated areas whenever possible.

  • Hide Valuables

    Never leave bags, electronics, or other valuables visible in your car.

  • Be Cautious Approaching

    Check surroundings before approaching your vehicle. Have keys ready before reaching the car.

  • Maintain Vehicle Security

    Ensure all windows are closed, alarms are activated, and tracking devices are functioning.

Community Awareness

Building safer communities together

  • Report Suspicious Activity

    Immediately report any unusual behavior or vehicles in your area to security or police.

  • Know Your Neighbors

    Build relationships with those around you. Watchful neighbors are a powerful crime deterrent.

  • Participate in Neighborhood Watch

    Join or start a neighborhood watch program. Collective vigilance improves everyone's safety.

  • Support Vulnerable Community Members

    Check on elderly or vulnerable neighbors regularly, especially during holidays or extreme weather.

Professional Security Is Essential

While these tips provide a strong foundation, professional security services offer comprehensive protection, rapid response, and peace of mind. Combine personal vigilance with professional security for complete safety.
